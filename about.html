About Us

House of Alden opened in July 2026, in a single room in Maur Mandi, Bathinda. Everything on this page is true, which is why it is short.

House of Alden is Kawalpreet Kaur and Parvinder Kaur. We registered as a micro-enterprise on 1 July 2026 — Udyam UDYAM-PB-03-0079079, should you wish to check — and began work the same week, in a room at Ward No. 5, near the dispensary in Maur Mandi, Bathinda. That room is still the whole of it: the drawing table, the stock, the packing bench, and the two of us.

We sell watches and earrings. Nothing else, and nothing else is planned. Between us we have spent years looking at old jewellery — the sort that surfaces in a family drawer, a stone short and still keeping time — and at some point looking stopped being enough.

So there is no workshop of forty hands behind this page, no archive, no founding decade to point at. There is a short list of pieces, an order book, and a WhatsApp number that one of us answers herself. If that sounds small, it is. It is also the entire reason the pieces are what they are.

On Materials

Our watches run on quartz movements. We say so plainly, because the alternative is to let you assume otherwise. In this room, at this price, a mechanical calibre would be a story rather than a fact. The cases are gold-tone or brushed steel, the dials enamel or matte, the straps calf leather and canvas. We leave the strap edges undyed: they look unfinished on the first day and considerably better by the hundredth.

The earrings are gold-plated brass, set with freshwater pearl and garnet. Plated brass is not solid gold, and you should hear that from us rather than work it out in a year. It is what lets a pair be worn on a Tuesday instead of kept in a locker for a wedding.

Every piece is assembled, checked and boxed here before it goes out. If one reaches you wrong, it reached you wrong from one of two people — and one of two people will put it right.

The pieces are the whole argument. There are not many of them yet.
